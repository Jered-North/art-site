Daughter of the Stars

This is a giginka of my new Imperial baby on Flight Rising, Astra.

I wanted her to have a childish look about her while still being womanly and graceful. This also was HIGHLY experimental as I was trying to figure out how to use matte medium.

The process is awesome, I used a bit of everything to pull this off. The layers are as follows:
1. Watercolor base in yellow ochre and thalo blue.
2. Colored pencil over that, filling in most of the colors.
3. Copic marker to blend, primarily greys, purples, a light blue, and a skin tone.
4. Acrylic paint- white, yellow, blue, and red. The acrylics were mixed and thinned with matte medium, the background was added except directly around the girl. Applied with fingers.
5. Gold acrylic ink to add the jewelery and brighten the golds, as well as added as stars in the back.
6. Chalk pastel used directly around the girl in a turquoise color, then blended with a darker blue pastel.
7. Added another layer of matte medium with fingers to seal the chalk pastel in place and blend it.
8. White colored pencil for highlights.
9. White gel pen for stars and highlights.
10. Added gold swirls in gold acrylic ink in the background.
11. Blue and black shading added with colored pencil to blend the background and help her stand out more. White colored pencil used to accent the swirls.
13. More chalk pastel used, dark blue and black.
14. Finally, glitter glue was applied with fingers- gold, turquoise, and silver.
15. Mounted on scrapbook paper that then was darkened digitally.
